title: Trafficators
id: 3499104
Trafficators are semaphore signals which when operated protrude from the bodywork of a motor vehicle to indicate its intention to turn in the direction indicated by the pointing signal Trafficators are often located at the door pillar br br br History br They first appeared in the s when they were actuated either mechanically or pneumatically In Alfredo Barrachini in Rome added electric lights inside the arms that turned on as they extended but operation was still by a cable system Electric operation came in when the Naillik Motor Signal Company of Boston added electric motor drive This system was superseded by two French inventors Gustave Deneef and Maurice Boisson who used a linear solenoid in The final complete system came in when Berlin based Max Ruhl and Ernst Neuman combined internal illumination and solenoid operation but see Gladstone Adams br The shape of the trafficator arm is closely based upon the shape of the semaphore signal arm used by the Royal Bavarian Railway beginning in The only difference from the railway arm is that it has only one blade of the rail signal s terminal arrowhead so that the retracted trafficator sits flush with the vehicle s exterior br They were common on vehicles until the introduction of the flashing amber red or white indicators at or near the corners of the vehicle and often along the sides as well They have been increasingly rare since the s as ever stricter legislation has prescribed the need for the modern type of flashing signal Many historic vehicles e g pre Volkswagen Beetle that are used on today s roads have had their trafficators supplemented or replaced with modern indicators to aid visibility and to meet legislative requirements In some countries trafficators may now be retro fitted with flashing lights br br br br br The Motor Car Its Evolution and Engineering Development John Day Bosch Ltd ISBN br br br External links br The dictionary definition of trafficator at Wiktionary
