title: Flemish Secession hoax
id: 8452915
Tout a ne nous rendra pas la Belgique or Bye Bye Belgium also called The Flemish Secession Hoax was a hoax perpetrated by the French language Belgian public TV station RTBF on Wednesday December Regular programming on the channel La Une was interrupted for a news bulletin claiming that the Flemish parliament had issued a unilateral declaration of independence from the Kingdom of Belgium mimicking the Belgian secession from the Netherlands some years earlier br Interviews with prominent Belgian politicians some of whom had been informed about the hoax as well as staged footage of the evacuation of the royal family as well as cheering crowds holding the Flag of Flanders were included so as to add credence to the news report br The broadcast of the report led to widespread alarm and consternation in French speaking Belgium A hotline set up by the station was swamped by calls whilst RTBF s website crashed Thirty minutes into the broadcast on demand of the media minister of the French speaking community in Belgium Fadila Laanan an on screen message identified it as fiction br The hoax was the creation of the journalist Philippe Dutilleul and had been prepared over a period of years under the codename BBB for Bye bye Belgium Its goal was to show Belgians the intensity of the country s issue and the real possibility of a split of Belgium In the years coming to the hoax there was rising Flemish separatism and the Vlaams Belang party received strong support in the regional elections br Prominent Belgian politicians including Prime Minister Guy Verhofstadt Flemish Minister President Yves Leterme and Walloon Minister President Elio Di Rupo condemned the report as irresponsible International reaction included an angry statement by Jean Claude Juncker Prime
