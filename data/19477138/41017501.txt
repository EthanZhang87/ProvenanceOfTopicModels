Gaming and Leisure Properties Inc is a real estate investment trust REIT specializing in casino properties based in Wyomissing Pennsylvania It was formed in November as a corporate spin off from Penn National Gaming The company owns casino properties all of which are leased to other companies br br br History br The company was created as a corporate spin off from Penn National Gaming now Penn Entertainment effective November The corporate breakup was designed to increase investor returns by taking advantage of the lack of federal income taxes on REITs br In November GLPI agreed to finance a proposed billion dollar casino in Milford Massachusetts but the project was killed days later when town voters rejected the casino br In January the company acquired the real estate assets of the Casino Queen in East St Louis Illinois for million and leased them back to the casino s operating company for million a year GLPI also loaned million to the casino br The company s Argosy Casino in Sioux City Iowa was forced to close in July and GLPI then sold the casino s real estate br In May GLPI agreed to buy The Meadows Racetrack and Casino in western Pennsylvania from Cannery Casino Resorts for million The company said it would sell the facility s license to a third party operator while retaining ownership of the land and buildings The deal ran into trouble with GLPI filing a lawsuit accusing Cannery of fraud in October the lawsuit was eventually settled with an agreement on a reduced purchase price of million br After casino operator Pinnacle Entertainment announced its own plan in November to spin off a REIT with the real estate assets of its casinos GLPI approached Pinnacle with an offer to buy those assets which it said would be simpler and faster than Pinnacle s plan Pinnacle did not respond to the offer so GLPI went public with its offer in March In July the companies reached a deal for GLPI to buy of Pinnacle s properties for billion in stock and lease them back to Pinnacle with rent starting at million per year The acquisition was completed in April GLPI also completed its purchase of The Meadows in September and sold the racetrack operation to Pinnacle for million br In May GLPI agreed to finance the real estate portion of a proposed million casino in New Bedford Massachusetts but the plan was canceled months later after developers failed to secure the rest of the needed funding br In May GLPI purchased the real estate assets of Bally s Casino Tunica and Resorts Casino Tunica for a total of million while Penn National simultaneously acquired their operating assets br In October the company acquired the real estate of five casinos from Tropicana Entertainment for million The purchase was part of a three way deal in which Eldorado Resorts simultaneously acquired Tropicana s operating business and leased the casinos from GLPI for a total of million per year br Weeks later GLPI completed a four way deal that saw its two largest tenants combine into one as Penn National acquired Pinnacle GLPI also gained a new tenant in Boyd Gaming which purchased the operations of three of Pinnacle s leased properties In connection with the merger GLPI acquired the real estate of Plainridge Park Casino from Penn National for million and lent million to Boyd to acquire the real estate of Belterra Park GLPI later acquired ownership of Belterra Park in satisfaction of the loan br In April during the COVID pandemic Penn National faced the prospect of financial issues brought on by resort closures As a result it sold the real estate of the Tropicana Las Vegas to GLPI for million in rent credits Penn National would continue to operate the Tropicana for another two years or until the resort were sold br GLPI took ownership of Lumi re
