Nexuiz is a free first person shooter video game developed and published by Alientrap The game was released under the GNU General Public License GPL and uses the DarkPlaces engine a modified Quake engine A remake also called Nexuiz was released for Steam and Xbox using CryEngine The original game was released on May br br br Gameplay br br Nexuiz is primarily multiplayer though it includes a full single player campaign which allows one to play through the various multiplayer game types and maps with bots and allows for hosting and joining of games It supports new gametypes or whole conversions quickly applied to it much like Quake br br br Development br Nexuiz development started as a Quake modification in the summer of by Lee Vermeulen Soon afterward the project moved to the DarkPlaces engine created by Ashley Hale who later also joined the project The original design called for a simple deathmatch project with a few levels and one character model to be released the next summer After four years of development with no budget Nexuiz was released on May completely under the GNU GPL and by the end of June had over a quarter million downloads Development continued after the initial release with released soon after released February released June and September On February nearly three years after the initial release version was released and brought major improvements to both the GUI and the graphics engine This includes all new GUI graphics elements as well as reflective water and improved particles In October a call was made for more developers for Nexuiz by the main and only QuakeC developer who identified organizational issues associated with many users Responses to this call highlighted the need for better documentation of QuakeC and the Nexuiz code while also acknowledging the difficulty that documentation of this placed on the small team of Nexuiz developers From mid November a number of people expressed interest in continuing development of Nexuiz Competitive play br GameStop locations across the US held an in store Nexuiz PC gaming challenge Interactive kiosks were set up in different stores in US cities The kiosks gave users minutes to earn the high score by doing the most damage possible to their AI opponents The highest monthly scorer in each location won a GameStop gift card There were new maps every month and each player was allowed one game per day Game play was open to GameStop customers ages and up and ages and above were eligible to win the monthly gift card for the high score Cities with the Challenge were Dallas Miami Chicago Boston Pittsburgh Seattle Los Angeles and San Francisco br br br Remake and fork br br On March it was announced the name Nexuiz had been licensed to IllFonic for commercial release This
