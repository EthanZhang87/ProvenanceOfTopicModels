Pixar RenderMan also known as RenderMan is a photorealistic D rendering software produced by Pixar Animation Studios Pixar uses RenderMan to render their in house D animated movie productions and it is also available as a commercial product licensed to third parties In a free non commercial version of RenderMan became available br br br Name br To speed up rendering Pixar engineers performed experiments with parallel rendering computers using Transputer chips inside a Pixar Image Computer The name comes from the nickname of a small circuit board inches or cm containing one Transputer that engineer Jeff Mock could put in his pocket During that time the Sony Walkman was very popular and Jeff Mock called his portable board Renderman leading to the software name br br br Technology br RenderMan defines cameras geometry materials and lights using the RenderMan Interface Specification This specification facilitates communication between D modeling and animation applications and the render engine that generates the final high quality images In the past RenderMan used the Reyes Rendering Architecture The Renderman standard was first presented at SIGGRAPH developed with input from companies and or big partners with Pat Hanrahan taking a leading role Ed Catmull said no software product met the RenderMan Standard in RenderMan met it after about two years br Additionally RenderMan supports Open Shading Language to define textural patterns br br When Pixar started development Steve Jobs described the original goal for RenderMan in Our goal is to make Renderman and Iceman the system software of the s Mr Jobs said likening these programs to PostScript the software developed by Adobe Systems Inc for high quality typography br During this time Pixar used the C language for developing Renderman which allowed them to port it to many platforms br Historically RenderMan used the Reyes algorithm to render images with added support for advanced effects such as ray tracing and global illumination Support for Reyes rendering and the RenderMan Shading Language were removed from RenderMan in br RenderMan currently uses Monte Carlo path tracing to generate images br br br
