 st Issue Special is a comics anthology series from DC Comics done in a similar style to their Showcase series It was published from April to April The goal was to showcase a new possible first issue of an ongoing series each month with some issues debuting new characters and others reviving dormant series from DC s past No series were actually launched from st Issue Special but the Warlord made his first appearance in the title and the character s ongoing series was already slated to debut a few months later br br br Publication history br Writer Gerry Conway explained the series origin st Issue Special was a peculiar book concept based on publisher Carmine Infantino s observation that first issues of titles often sold better than subsequent issues Carmine s brainstorm a monthly series of nothing but first issues It sounds like a joke but he was dead serious br Conway has also denied that st Issue Special was a tryout series pointing out that tryout series run each feature for several issues so that the publisher has enough time to get sales figures before deciding whether to give the feature its own series since each feature in st Issue Special ran only one issue DC would have had to either launch the new series before sales figures came in for the tryout thus making the feature s appearance in st Issue Special pointless or launch the new series six months or more after the tryout issue by which time reader interest in the feature would have faded Conway added We used to sit at editorial meetings and Carmine Infantino would say Who has an idea for st Issue Special next month How do you develop a project that has a potential to be a real series within days You can t Only two of the st Issue Special features received an ongoing series Mike Grell s The Warlord which first appeared in issue November and Gerry Conway and Mike Vosburg s Return of the New Gods which appeared in issue br Issues featuring Atlas featured art and story by Jack Kirby A number of issues featured existing DC characters issue Metamorpho issue Manhunter issue the Creeper issue the Golden Age character Doctor Fate and issue the New Gods The Metamorpho feature reunited the character s creators writer Bob Haney and artist Ramona Fradon Haney and Fradon had met at the San Diego Comic Con and while reminiscing it emerged that both of them regarded Metamorpho as one of the features they had most enjoyed working on leading them to ask DC if they could do one more Metamorpho story together st Issue Special staff have not been able to answer why the Creeper story was illustrated but not written by the character s creator Steve Ditko br Issue featured a new Starman character which would later be used in James Robinson s s series focused on the character Jack Knight Robin team up was published in Batman Family September October and a Green Arrow and Black Canary story was kept in inventory until it was published as a backup feature in Green Lantern January br st Issue Special never printed a letters column opting instead to accompany each feature with a Story Behind the Story text page br DC published a hard cover collection of the series in br Characters
