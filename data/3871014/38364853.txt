Brady John Haran born June is an Australian British independent filmmaker and video journalist who produces educational videos and documentary films for his YouTube channels the most notable being Computerphile and Numberphile Haran is also the co host of the Hello Internet podcast along with fellow educational YouTuber CGP Grey On August Haran launched his second podcast called The Unmade Podcast and on November he launched his third podcast The Numberphile Podcast based on his mathematics centered channel of the same name br br br Reporter and filmmaker br Brady Haran studied journalism for a year before being hired by The Adelaide Advertiser In he moved from Australia to Nottingham United Kingdom In Nottingham he worked for the BBC began to work with film and reported for East Midlands Today BBC News Online and BBC radio stations br In Haran worked as a filmmaker in residence for Nottingham Science City as part of an agreement between the BBC and the University of Nottingham His Test Tube project started with the idea of producing a documentary about scientists and their research but he decided to upload his raw footage to YouTube from that point Periodic Videos and Sixty Symbols were developed Haran then left the BBC to work full time making YouTube videos br br br YouTube channels br Following Test Tube Haran decided to create new YouTube channels In
