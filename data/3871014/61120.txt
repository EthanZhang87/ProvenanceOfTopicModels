A Florence flask boiling flask is a type of flask used as an item of laboratory glassware and is named after the city Florence It is used as a container to hold liquids A Florence flask has a round body a long neck and often a flat bottom It is designed for uniform heating boiling distillation and ease of swirling it is produced in a number of different glass thicknesses to stand different types of use They are often made of borosilicate glass for heat and chemical resistance Traditional Florence flasks typically do not have a ground glass joint on their rather longer necks but typically have a slight lip or flange around the tip of the neck The common volume for a Florence flask is litre br br br See also br Erlenmeyer flask br Round bottom flask br br br
