id: 301007
The gunwale is the top edge of the hull of a ship or boat br Originally the structure was the gun wale on a sailing warship a horizontal reinforcing band added at and above the level of a gun deck to offset the stresses created by firing artillery Over time it remained as a valuable stiffener mounted inboard of the sheer strake on commercial and recreational craft In modern boats it is the top edge of the hull where there is usually some form of stiffening often in the form of traditional wooden boat construction members called the inwale and outwale br On a canoe the gunwale is typically the widened edge at the top of its hull reinforced with wood plastic or aluminum to carry the thwarts br On a narrowboat or canal boat the gunwale is synonymous with the side deck a narrow ledge running the full length of the craft br br br
