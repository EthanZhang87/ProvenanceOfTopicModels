title: Beakhead
id: 12777930
A beakhead or beak is the protruding part of the foremost section of a sailing ship It was fitted on sailing vessels from the th to the th century and served as a working platform by sailors working the sails of the bowsprit the forward pointing mast that carries the spritsails The beakhead would be one of the most ornate sections of a ship particularly in the extravagant Baroque style ships of the th century The sides were often decorated with carved statues and located directly underneath was the figurehead usually in the form of animals shields or mythological creatures The beakhead also housed the crew s toilets head which would drop refuse straight into the sea without sullying the ship s hull unnecessarily br br br
