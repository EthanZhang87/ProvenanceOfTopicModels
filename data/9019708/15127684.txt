title: Orwell (programming language)
id: 15127684
Orwell is a small lazy evaluation functional programming language implemented principally by Martin Raskovsky and first released in by Philip Wadler during his time as a Research Fellow in the Programming Research Group part of the Oxford University Computing Laboratory Developed as a free alternative to Miranda it was a forerunner of Haskell and was one of the first programming languages to support list comprehensions and pattern matching br The name is a tribute to George Orwell s novel Nineteen Eighty Four the year in which the programming language was released In the late s and the s most of the computing practical assignments for undergraduates studying for a degree in Mathematics and Computation at Oxford University were required to be completed using the language br br br br Wadler P L et al Introduction to Orwell Programming Research Group of Oxford University cite journal Cite journal requires journal help
