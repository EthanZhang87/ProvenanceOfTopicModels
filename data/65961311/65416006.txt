The NHL Entry Draft was the th NHL Entry Draft The draft was held on July at the Bell Centre in Montreal Quebec Montreal were originally scheduled to host the draft but were unable to do so due to the COVID pandemic For the first time since the team hosting the draft selected first overall br The first three selections were Juraj Slafkovsky going to the hosting Montreal Canadiens Simon Nemec being selected by the New Jersey Devils and Logan Cooley being picked by the Arizona Coyotes The selections of Slovak players Slafkovsky and Nemec made this the second time that the top two draftees came from a single European country after Russians Alexander Ovechkin and Evgeni Malkin in br br br Eligibility br Ice hockey players born between January and September were eligible for selection in the NHL Entry Draft Additionally un drafted non North American players born in were eligible for the draft and those players who were drafted in the NHL Entry Draft but not signed by an NHL team and who were born after June were also eligible to re enter the draft br br br Draft lottery br br Beginning with the NHL season the NHL changed the weighting system that was used in previous years Under the new system the odds of winning the draft lottery for the four lowest finishing teams in the league decreased while the odds for the other non playoff teams increased As the league reduced the number of lottery drawings before the season this resulted in two lotteries being held for this draft Beginning with this lottery and continuing onward the teams winning one of the two drawings are allowed to move up a maximum of ten spots in the draft order and a team is only allowed to win the lottery twice in a five year period As a result only the eleven lowest finishing teams were eligible to win the first overall selection The team that won the first drawing would move up to the first selection if they were seeded teams seeded would move up ten spots If the team seeded won the first draw they would secure the second overall pick and the team seeded first would secure the first overall pick For the second draw the fifteen remaining teams were re seeded and the teams seeded could move up to the second overall pick if it was still available after the end of the first draw teams seeded could move up a maximum of ten spots If the second overall pick was not available to the winner of the second draw that team would be awarded the next highest available selection After the two draws were complete all remaining draft picks were assigned to the remaining teams in inverse order of regular season finish br The Montreal Canadiens and New Jersey Devils won the two draft lotteries that took place on May giving them the first and second picks overall Montreal retained the first pick while New Jersey moved up three spots Arizona Seattle and Philadelphia each dropped one spot to third fourth and fifth overall respectively br br br Top prospects br Source NHL Central Scouting May ranking
