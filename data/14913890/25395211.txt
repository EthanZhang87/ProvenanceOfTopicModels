The V engine is a V engine with two cylinders in one bank and one cylinder in the other bank It is a rare configuration which has been mostly used in two stroke engines for motorcycles competing in Grand Prix motorcycle racing br The first example was the DKW The Suzuki RP was intended to compete in the season however a rule change mandating single cylinder engines meant that the cc cu in RP never raced br Honda later revived the layout for the Honda NS NSR Grand Prix racing motorcycles The Honda MVX F and Honda NS R sports bikes also used V engines br A related layout was the W engine although this placed all three cylinders in the same plane but none of them in the same bank This was used for the four stroke Anzani engine which was used in motorcycle and aircraft applications br br br See also br W engine br br br
