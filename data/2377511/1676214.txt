A pocket cruiser is a sailboat designed for recreational cruising and club racing under feet m in length br Like the similar and usually smaller trailer sailer they have design features such as light weight and short ballasted retractable shoal draft keels that allow them to be towed by passenger vehicles br Being cruisers they also include amenities that provide the comfort of larger boats such as a cabin with berths a galley a head and cockpit br Properly equipped these style boats are capable of long offshore passages as proven by circumnavigators Eric and Susan Hiscock Lin and Larry Pardey and Tania Aebi among others br The term was coined in the s as fiberglass sailing yacht building took off br br br See also br Day sailer br br br Notes
