Arthur John Robin Gorell Milner January March was a British computer scientist and a Turing Award winner br br br Life education and career br Milner was born in Yealmpton near Plymouth England into a military family He gained a King s Scholarship to Eton College in and was awarded the Tomline Prize the highest prize in Mathematics at Eton in Subsequently he served in the Royal Engineers attaining the rank of Second Lieutenant He then enrolled at King s College Cambridge graduating in Milner first worked as a schoolteacher then as a programmer at Ferranti before entering academia at City University London then Swansea University Stanford University and from at the University of Edinburgh where he was a co founder of the Laboratory for Foundations of Computer Science LFCS He returned to Cambridge as the head of the Computer Laboratory in from which he eventually stepped down although he was still at the laboratory From Milner was a Scottish Informatics Computer Science Alliance Advanced Research Fellow and held part time the Chair of Computer Science at the University of Edinburgh br Milner died of a heart attack on March in Cambridge His wife Lucy died shortly before he did br br br Contributions br Milner is generally regarded as having made three major contributions to computer science He developed Logic for Computable Functions LCF one of the first tools for automated theorem proving The language he developed for LCF ML was the first language with polymorphic type inference and type safe exception handling In a very different area Milner also developed a theoretical framework for analyzing concurrent systems the calculus of communicating systems CCS and its successor the calculus br At the time of his death he was working on bigraphs a formalism for ubiquitous computing subsuming CCS and the calculus He is also credited for rediscovering the Hindley Milner type system br br br Honors and awards br He was made a Fellow of the Royal Society and a Distinguished Fellow of the British Computer Society in Milner received the ACM Turing Award in In he was inducted as a Fellow of the ACM In the Royal Society of Edinburgh awarded Milner with a Royal Medal for his bringing about public benefits on a global scale In he was elected a Foreign Associate of the National Academy of Engineering for fundamental contributions to computer science including the development of LCF ML CCS and the calculus br The Royal Society Milner Award br and the ACM SIGPLAN Robin Milner Young Researcher Award are both named after him br br br Selected publications br A
